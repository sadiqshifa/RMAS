HMDA Reportability Determination

Walks a depository or non-depository institution through the coverage tests under Regulation C to determine whether it must report mortgage data for the current collection year.

12 CFR §1003.2(g) 2026 asset threshold: $59,000,000 Effective through July 1, 2026 (eCFR)

Depository Institution Test

Banks, savings associations, and credit unions. All five conditions under §1003.2(g)(1) must be satisfied for the institution to be covered.

1. Asset-Size Threshold§1003.2(g)(1)(i)
Assets exceeded the annually-adjusted threshold as of the preceding December 31.
2. Location§1003.2(g)(1)(ii)
Had a home or branch office in a Metropolitan Statistical Area as of the preceding December 31.
3. Loan Activity§1003.2(g)(1)(iii)
Originated at least one home purchase loan (or refinancing of one) secured by a first lien on a 1–4 unit dwelling, in the preceding calendar year.
4. Federally Related§1003.2(g)(1)(iv)
Federally insured or regulated, or the loan in Test 3 was insured/guaranteed by a federal agency, or intended for sale to Fannie Mae or Freddie Mac.
5. Loan-Volume Threshold§1003.2(g)(1)(v)
At least 25 closed-end mortgage loans in each of the two preceding years, OR at least 200 open-end lines of credit in each of the two preceding years.

Non-Depository Institution Test

For-profit mortgage lenders that are not banks, savings associations, or credit unions. Only two conditions apply under §1003.2(g)(2) — no asset-size or federally-related test.

1. Location§1003.2(g)(2)(i)
Satisfied either by having a home or branch office in an MSA, or — for institutions with no physical branch — by having received applications for, originated, or purchased 5 or more covered loans related to property in an MSA in the preceding calendar year.
2. Loan-Volume Threshold§1003.2(g)(2)(ii)
At least 25 closed-end mortgage loans in each of the two preceding years, OR at least 200 open-end lines of credit in each of the two preceding years.

Asset-Size Threshold History

The depository asset-size threshold (§1003.2(g)(1)(i)) is adjusted annually based on the change in CPI-W for the 12-month period ending in November. This value is hardcoded in Tab 1 for the current collection year — the exact kind of figure a reg-change-monitor process needs to catch before it goes stale.
