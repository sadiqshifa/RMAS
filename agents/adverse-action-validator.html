Adverse Action Notice Validator

Checks a draft adverse action notice's timing and required content against Regulation B, and flags language that may reference a legal standard the CFPB removed as of July 21, 2026.

12 CFR §1002.9 15 U.S.C. §1691 et seq. Final rule eff. July 21, 2026
Why this date matters: on April 22, 2026 the CFPB removed the "effects test" from Regulation B and stated ECOA does not recognize disparate-impact liability as a standalone theory — disparate treatment remains actionable. It also narrowed the discouragement prohibition to statements of intent to discriminate, and barred for-profit creditors from using race, color, national origin, or sex as a common eligibility characteristic in Special Purpose Credit Programs. A notice or SPCP program drafted before April 2026 may still reference the old standard.

Litigation status: consumer advocacy groups and state attorneys general are widely expected to challenge this rule, and several legal trackers consider a preliminary injunction before the July 21 effective date plausible. As of this tool's last update, no injunction had been confirmed and the rule stands as scheduled — but that status can change before the effective date itself. Verify current status before relying on any determination from this tool.

Credit extension timing§1002.8 — transition rule
The new rule applies prospectively. SPCP credit extended before July 21, 2026 stays governed by the rule in place when the program was established and that credit was extended. Credit extended on or after July 21, 2026 must comply with the amended rule, regardless of when the program itself was originally established.
